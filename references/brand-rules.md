# Brand Rules

Use these rules when creating `/glyph brand` or `/glyph kit` outputs.

## Positioning

- State who the product is for.
- State the transformation the product creates.
- State what the product should never feel like.
- Make the category clear before adding personality.

## Logo Direction

- Match the logo idea to the product category.
- Prefer simple, ownable marks over decorative symbols.
- Avoid generic sparkles, abstract blobs, and meaningless initials.
- Explain why the logo direction fits the audience and product behavior.

## Color

- Give each primary color a job.
- Avoid choosing colors only because they feel trendy.
- Include neutrals and surface colors, not only accents.
- Explain emotional intent and interface use.

## Typography

- Use type to create hierarchy, not just style.
- Pair fonts with contrast in role, weight, or personality.
- Avoid default startup pairings unless they clearly fit.
- Describe headline, body, label, and UI text usage.

## Voice

- Keep copy specific to the product.
- Avoid vague phrases like "supercharge your workflow."
- Make the tone match the buyer, user, and category.

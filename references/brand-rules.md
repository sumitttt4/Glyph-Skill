# Brand Rules

Use these rules when creating `/glyph brand` or `/glyph kit` outputs.

## Positioning

- State who the product is for.
- State the transformation the product creates.
- State what the product should never feel like.
- Make the category clear before adding personality.
- Choose one primary promise and make every visual decision support it.
- Avoid category confusion. A fintech, developer tool, wellness app, and AI writing assistant should not use the same brand signals.

## Logo Direction

- Match the logo idea to the product category.
- Prefer simple, ownable marks over decorative symbols.
- Avoid generic sparkles, abstract blobs, and meaningless initials.
- Explain why the logo direction fits the audience and product behavior.

## Wordmarks vs Symbols

- Use a wordmark when the name is distinctive, short, or the product needs clarity before memorability.
- Use a symbol when the product behavior can be reduced to a recognizable metaphor, motion, tool, or category signal.
- Use a monogram only when the initials create a shape with meaning or a strong visual rhythm.
- Avoid symbols that could belong to any AI startup.

## Color

- Give each primary color a job.
- Avoid choosing colors only because they feel trendy.
- Include neutrals and surface colors, not only accents.
- Explain emotional intent and interface use.
- Map colors to product behavior: trust, speed, craft, analysis, calm, precision, creativity, or urgency.
- Define primary, accent, background, surface, border, muted text, and semantic colors when useful.

## Typography

- Use type to create hierarchy, not just style.
- Pair fonts with contrast in role, weight, or personality.
- Avoid default startup pairings unless they clearly fit.
- Describe headline, body, label, and UI text usage.
- Pair expressive display type with restrained UI type when the brand needs personality.
- Pair one strong sans family with disciplined weight and size rules when the product should feel focused and operational.
- Avoid Poppins-only design unless the product truly needs a round, soft, mainstream startup tone.

## Voice

- Keep copy specific to the product.
- Avoid vague phrases like "supercharge your workflow."
- Make the tone match the buyer, user, and category.
- Define what the brand sounds like when explaining, warning, selling, and guiding.
- Prefer concrete nouns and verbs over generic startup abstractions.

## Industry-Specific Brand Decisions

- Fintech should signal trust, clarity, restraint, and risk awareness.
- Developer tools should signal precision, speed, composability, and strong documentation.
- Creator tools should signal expression, momentum, and low friction.
- Analytics products should signal confidence, legibility, and decision quality.
- AI products should make the human outcome clear instead of relying on sparkle motifs.
- Healthcare and wellness products should signal calm, care, accessibility, and safety.
- Enterprise products should feel durable and organized without becoming sterile.
